Experience

Real projects, real clients, real constraints.

My professional experience spans HVAC system design, mechanical load analysis, energy compliance, and client-facing engineering across commercial, tech campus, lab, and data center projects.

ALFATECH

Mechanical Designer, E.I.T

June 2024 - July 2025

  • Performed room-by-room and system-level heating and cooling load analyses across commercial, hospitality, lab, and tech campus projects using HAP and Trane TRACE, deriving building envelope inputs directly from architectural drawings and applying California Mechanical Code assumptions alongside project-specific design standards. On a full Hilton first-floor renovation, inherited a load calculation in progress and restructured it so thoroughly across every room that the final model was effectively rebuilt from the ground up.
  • Designed HVAC systems against real physical, budgetary, and client constraints rather than ideal conditions. On a Hilton renovation, the client was unwilling to upgrade every room, so I continuously coordinated with them to identify exactly which spaces would receive new diffusers and fan coil units, shaping the entire design scope around what they would actually approve. On a separate Google project, the originally specified rooftop unit could not fit the existing roof curb, so I worked through equipment submittals from multiple vendors to find the unit that best matched the performance requirements while fitting within the physical footprint.
  • Accounted for field conditions that drawings alone could not capture. On a lab project with no existing plans, I surveyed the roof to locate every exhaust fan, traced the duct network back through the building, and reverse-engineered the system layout before a single line was drawn, allowing fume hood placement and exhaust routing to be designed around what was actually there. On a Google refrigerant compliance project, I walked the entire building to verify that documentation matched field conditions before the redesign work began.
  • Designed outdoor air distribution to meet zone-by-zone ventilation requirements and adapt to new occupancy conditions. On the Hilton project this included diverting outside air to spaces where occupant density had increased under the new layout, and redirecting cooling capacity to zones that would have become heat-loaded from new equipment such as vending machines. Full-height partition walls that altered plenum airflow paths were incorporated into the design rather than ignored.
  • Audited live building systems using operational data. On a Google campus, I pulled BMS data to extract actual supply CFM for every air handler in the building, identified units running below design airflow, and cross-validated the findings with an independent Trane TRACE model built using Google's internal engineering rulebook and the California Mechanical Code where the rulebook did not specify.
  • Delivered permit-ready mechanical drawing sets in AutoCAD and Revit, maintained revision-controlled sets on data center projects where client floor plans changed frequently enough to require continuous redraw cycles, and completed Title 24 energy compliance documentation in EnergyCodeAce across multiple projects.
